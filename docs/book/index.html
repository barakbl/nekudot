Nekudot Open the app → Book

Draw with connections, not just strokes

Nekudot is a browser-based expressive drawing tool. As you draw, it remembers every point and weaves faint lines between nearby ones - so a single gesture blooms into a web of its own. Inspired by mrdoob's Harmony.

The idea

Most brushes only lay down the pixels under your cursor. Nekudot also gives your drawing a memory - it remembers everywhere you've drawn. While you draw, a connecting brush reaches out to nearby remembered spots and draws faint lines between them. Hundreds of those overlapping threads build the soft, sketchy, spider-web texture that Harmony made famous.

Because that memory is shared and sticks around, your marks relate to each other: new strokes reach back toward old ones, and the drawing grows as one connected thing rather than a stack of separate lines.

Try this first Open the app, grab Web (1) and scribble a loose shape - then scribble a second one nearby and watch faint threads leap between them. That bloom is the whole idea; everything else just shapes how it looks.

Features

  • Connecting brushes - Web weaves threads between your points; pick a connection style for the look.
  • Connections - styles like Classic, Web, Fur & Lace; tune where the lines go and how they look (alpha, inset, dash, grain…).
  • Symmetry - repeat any brush across a Tile lattice, a Radial kaleidoscope, or a single Mirror line.
  • Pen & pressure - a stylus shapes every mark: pressure and tilt drive size, opacity, even the web; the Marker's chisel follows the pen's lean.
  • Shape brush - Shapes stamps speed-sized squares or circles (pick which in its settings).
  • Colour palettes - pick from palettes grouped by category, mix in OKLCH/HSB, and import or export GIMP .gpl files.
  • Layers - stack canvases with opacity; drag to reorder, duplicate & delete.
  • Memory maps - what your drawing remembers; feed or reuse them across layers.
  • Save & load - export a flat PNG, or a .nekudot archive you can reopen and keep editing.
  • Save to a folder (Chrome/Edge) - connect a folder and save artwork & settings straight to disk; point it at a synced folder for your own cloud backup.
  • Record GIF - capture a few seconds of drawing, then preview it, change the speed and trim the ends before saving the .gif.
  • Runs entirely in the browser - your work is stored locally; nothing is uploaded.
