Navigating the canvas

Pan, zoom and rotate your view of the canvas. None of it changes the art - only how you look at it.

What it is

Nekudot puts a camera over the canvas. You can slide it around, zoom in for detail, and (on touch) spin it to a comfortable angle - handy for drawing on a phone or tablet. The artwork itself is untouched, and Reset view always brings you back. The canvas also auto-fits on load and whenever the window shrinks, so a canvas bigger than the window never leaves your work stranded off-screen.

On a computer

Zoom
⌘/Ctrl + scroll wheel - zooms toward the pointer.
Pan
Scroll (or a two-finger trackpad swipe) to slide the view, or drag with the middle mouse button.
Reset view
The frame button in the toolbar - back to 100%, no rotation, centred or fit.

On a touch screen

One finger
Draws, as usual.
Two-finger drag
Pan, pinch to zoom, and twist to rotate - all at once, around the point between your fingers.
Two-finger tap
Undo (a tap, not a drag); three-finger tap is Redo.
Tap vs drag. A quick two-finger tap undoes; the moment your fingers move it becomes a pan / zoom / rotate instead, so the two never collide.
Try this Zoom in close to clean up a detail, then hit Reset view to snap back to the whole piece. Lost in a big canvas? Reset view re-centres it.
