Nekudot is a drawing tool with a memory. Set down a mark and it keeps the points; draw nearby and faint threads leap across the gap. One gesture blooms into a web of its own.

Why it feels different

Most brushes only lay down the pixels under your cursor, then forget them. Nekudot gives your drawing a memory - it holds on to everywhere you've been. While you draw, a connecting brush reaches toward nearby remembered points and threads faint lines between them. Hundreds of those overlapping threads build the soft, sketchy bloom that drawing tools like Harmony made beloved.

It remembers

Every point you set down stays. Your drawing always has somewhere to reach back toward - it grows as one connected thing, not a stack of separate lines.

It connects

As your hand moves, threads stretch to nearby points and fill the space between them. That bloom is the whole idea; everything else just shapes how it looks.

It stays yours

Everything runs in your browser. Your work is saved on your own machine - nothing is uploaded, nothing is watched. Just you and the canvas.

In the studio

Everything you need to wander

  • Connecting brushes - Round weaves a web from your points; pick a style for the mood.
  • Connection styles - Classic, Web, Arc, Fur & Lace. Tune where threads land and how they fall.
  • Symmetry - echo any mark across a tile lattice, a radial kaleidoscope, or a single mirror line.
  • Pen & pressure - a stylus shapes every mark: pressure and tilt drive size, opacity, even the web.
  • Shape brushes - squares & circles that grow with the speed of your hand.
  • Layers & memory maps - stack your work, and decide what the drawing remembers.
  • Keep your work - export a flat PNG, or a .nekudot archive you can reopen and keep going.
