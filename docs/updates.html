Updates

What's new.

The latest brushes, tools and touches - in plain language, for the people who draw with Nekudot. For the technical log, see the changelog.

June 23, 2026v0.6.0

Keep your memory map in sight

Drawing toward a remembered shape used to mean flashing your map every few seconds just to check where its points were. Now you can keep it lit. One tap on the maps button pins the active map's dots so they glow softly on the canvas while you work - you always see what your strokes are reaching for, without breaking your flow.

  • Pin the active map's points so they stay visible as you draw - tap again to hide them
  • It still flashes the moment you turn it on, so you spot the points right away
  • Choose any highlight colour from the Maps panel - it tints both the pinned dots and the flash

June 23, 2026v0.5.0

Bolder colours, palettes you can carry

Gradients now blend the way your eye expects - smooth and vivid, with no muddy grey in the middle - and your whole colour library finally travels with you. Build palettes, group them by category, and carry the lot between devices as a single file.

  • Smooth OKLCH gradients stay rich from end to end (toggle it in Settings)
  • One tidy Import / Export window for palettes, grouped by category
  • Back up and restore your entire palette collection as a single file

June 22, 2026v0.4.0

Reach further across the page

Connecting brushes can now throw their threads much further. The Reach dial goes all the way to 1000 px - five times its old limit - so big, sweeping webs that span the whole canvas are suddenly easy to draw.

  • Raise Reach up to 1000 px for canvas-spanning webs
  • Made for large canvases and loose, airy compositions
