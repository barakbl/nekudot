Grid brushes

Brushes that lay their mark on a regular grid instead of a free continuous stroke - stamps and tiled motifs that build up patterns.

What they are

Grid brushes snap to a configurable X / Y spacing. A single click (a tap) affects every grid intersection within reach of the cursor, with strength fading toward the edge of the reach circle. Tap repeatedly to build density. They're ideal for textures, fills and repeating patterns rather than line work.

The grid brushes

Dots - stamps a dot at every grid intersection in reach. The classic fill/stipple tool.

Lines - stamps a short angled line at each intersection (set the angle & length) for hatching and weave-like textures.

Handfree - mirrors a freehand round-with-connections motif across every grid junction, so one drawn mark tiles into wallpaper.

Ellipse is also a grid-tap brush; it lives directly under Brushes because it stamps a single shape rather than a pattern motif. The shared grid dials (X / Y spacing, Reach, Tap strength, Opacity falloff) work the same across all of them.
