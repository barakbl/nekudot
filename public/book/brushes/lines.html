Lines key 5

A grid-tap brush that stamps short angled strokes - like hatching.

What it does

Lines works like Dots, but stamps a short line segment at each grid intersection instead of a dot. All segments share the same angle and length, so a tap lays down a field of parallel hatch marks that fades toward the edge of the reach.

How to use

  • Press 5 or pick it from the brush menu.
  • Click to drop a patch of hatching; overlap taps at different angles for cross-hatching.

Settings

Angle
Direction of every segment (0° = horizontal).
Size
Length of each segment.
X spacing / Y spacing
Grid spacing between segments.
Reach
Radius around the click that gets stamped, with opacity falloff.
Tap strength
Maximum opacity at the center of a tap.
Opacity falloff
How sharply opacity fades toward the edge of the reach - 0 = flat, higher = steeper.
Try this Lay one patch of hatching, change the Angle by 60-90°, then tap over the same area again - the layers cross into rich cross-hatching that reads as shadow. Keep the strength low so each pass adds tone gradually.
