Draw with connections, not just strokes

Nekudot is a browser-based expressive drawing tool. As you draw, it remembers every point and weaves faint lines between nearby ones - so a single gesture blooms into a web of its own. Inspired by mrdoob's Harmony.

The idea

Most brushes only lay down the pixels under your cursor. Nekudot also keeps a point cloud - a "memory map" of everywhere your strokes have been. While you draw, a connecting brush looks up the neighbours of each new point in that cloud and draws lines between them. Hundreds of faint, overlapping lines accumulate into the soft, sketchy, spider-web texture that Harmony made famous.

Because the connections come from a shared, persistent cloud, strokes relate to each other: new marks reach back toward old ones, and the drawing grows as a single connected structure rather than a stack of independent lines.

Features

  • Connecting brushes - Round & Handfree weave a web from your points; pick a connection style for the look.
  • Connections - styles like Classic, Web, Arc, Fur & Lace; tune where the lines go and how they look (alpha, inset, dash, grain…).
  • Grid brushes - Dots, Lines & Handfree stamp patterns at grid intersections.
  • Shape brushes - Squares & Circles size themselves to your speed.
  • Layers - stack canvases with opacity; drag to reorder, duplicate & delete.
  • Memory maps - top-level point clouds you can read from / write to independently.
  • Save & load - export a flat PNG, or a .nekudot archive you can reopen and keep editing.
  • Runs entirely in the browser - your work is stored locally; nothing is uploaded.
