# Code of Conduct

Nekudot is made by developers and artists, for developers and artists. This
project welcomes both, and everyone in between. This Code of Conduct sets out
what we expect of one another so that taking part - writing code, drawing,
filing an issue, or just asking a question - stays a good experience for all.

It applies to everyone in the community: contributors, maintainers, artists,
and anyone interacting in our spaces.

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in Nekudot a harassment-free experience for
everyone, regardless of age, body, disability, ethnicity, sex, gender identity
and expression, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or any other
characteristic - and regardless of how much (or how little) experience you have
as a developer or as an artist.

We are building a tool for creativity. We want the community around it to feel
just as open and encouraging as a blank canvas.

## What we value

- **Respect for contributors and artists alike.** Code and art are both real
  work. A pull request, a bug report, a drawing shared with the community, and a
  thoughtful question all deserve the same courtesy and good faith.
- **No racism, no discrimination, no harassment.** Racist, sexist, ableist, or
  otherwise discriminatory language and behaviour are never acceptable. Neither
  are slurs, demeaning jokes, or personal attacks.
- **Every background is welcome.** It does not matter whether you are a seasoned
  engineer or wrote your first line of code yesterday, a trained artist or
  someone who just likes making marks on a screen. Beginners are not a burden;
  helping them is part of the point.
- **Respect copyright and other people's work.** Only contribute work that is
  yours to give, or that you have the right to share. Do not copy code, art,
  assets, fonts, or text from others without permission and proper credit, and
  respect the licenses of everything you build on. Give credit where it is due.
- **Assume good intent, and be kind.** Disagree about ideas, not about people.
  Offer feedback that helps someone improve, and accept feedback gracefully.

## Originality and credit

Nekudot is a place to make and share your own art and code. To keep it that way:

- Only share work you created or have the right to share.
- Do not submit art, code, assets, fonts, or text that infringes someone else's
  copyright or other rights, and do not pass off others' work as your own -
  including work copied or laundered through other tools.
- When you build on, trace, or take inspiration from someone else's work, credit
  them and honour the license of whatever you use.

If you are unsure whether something is okay to share, ask first.

## Unacceptable behaviour

Examples of behaviour that will not be tolerated include:

- Discriminatory, racist, or harassing comments, jokes, imagery, or conduct.
- Personal attacks, insults, trolling, or deliberately derailing discussions.
- Belittling someone for their skill level, background, or the kind of work
  (code or art) they do.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Passing off someone else's work as your own, or contributing material that
  infringes copyright or other rights.
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances of any kind.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces - the repository, issues,
pull requests, discussions, and any community channels - and when an individual
is representing the project in public spaces.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, or have any concerns,
please report it to the maintainer at **barak.bloch@gmail.com**. All reports are
treated as confidential, and we aim to acknowledge them within a few days. You
will not be expected to share details with anyone you are not comfortable with,
and your privacy will be respected.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and upholding these standards. They
may take any action they deem appropriate, up to and including removing comments,
commits, code, edits, issues, and contributions, and temporarily or permanently
banning anyone for behaviour they consider inappropriate, threatening, or
harmful.
